1. Introduction

Broadly speaking, vision is interested in going from A to B where A, B {2D, 3D, Meaning/information/actions}. For example, meaning 3D is generative AI.

Teaching Goals

  1. Teach physical foundations of vision: Image formation, camera models, 3D geometry
  2. Teach classical vision algorithms that deepen understanding of key processes: multi-view geometry, optical flow, Fourier analysis
  3. Teach advanced deep learning fundamentals relevant to perception: generative modeling, representation learning, geometric deep learning
  4. Teach and reflect on “what’s next”, the cutting edge of research that lives at the intersection of vision, robotics, and Al

Classical Vision Tasks:

  • 2D images meaning

    • Objective/person/scene/action recognition/classification
    • Semantic segmentation
    • Semantic embedding images

  • 2D 3D

    • 3D reconstruction, e.g. shape from shading (assume Lambertian)
    • Depth

  • Intermediate tasks

    • Image enhancement, denoising, deblurring, etc.
    • Edge detection
    • Segmentation
    • Optical flow
    • 3D data for robotics

The classical perspective involved first principle derivations, geometry, physics, etc. Hand design features and filters for edge detections, etc. Hope that they are invariant to external factors like lighting and viewpoint. Wasn’t enough data to train DL. Breakthrough with AlexNet yields 2D meaning giving more data.

Modern AI Revolution:

  • Convergence of all modalities
  • Still a fair amount of traditional vision as a pre-process.
  • 2D 3D is still traditional (e.g. Gaussian splatting uses a combination of old-style multi view stero and analysis by synthesis.)
  • “Finding the right question is at least half of the job of a good researcher”.

History:

  • 1960-1970: Blocks (discrete objects, surfaces, and boundaries), Edges and Model Fitting
  • 1970-1981: Low-level vision: stereo, flow, shape-from-x
  • 1985-1988: Neural Networks, backprop., self-driving
  • 1990-2000: Dense stereo and multi-view stereo, MRFs
  • 2000-2010: Features, descriptors, structure-from-motion.
  • 2010-20??: Learning, deep learning, large datasets, rapid growth

Remarks

  • Deep learning struggled with tracking optical flow (tracking where pixels are going temporically.)
  • Photometric Stereo1 = recover 3D from multiple 2D images of the same pose with varying lightning
  • (Covered later in Section 8.1) Essential matrix answers: “Given how these two cameras are positioned relative to each other, here is the rule that all matching points must satisfy.”
  • Neural Fields (2020) handle scenes that would not be represented well with polygons
  • Gaussian spattling (2024): raycasting through 3D is wasteful. Instead represent the scene as a collection of points with size (3D gaussians, mean plus covariance) + color + opacity. No deep learning, just optimize difference between gaussians and images given camera orientation.
  • Generative models can be used for predicting actions for robotics.
  • Vision hasn’t yet reached the bitter lesson. Still uses concpets like multiview geometry, point clouds, equivariance, etc.
