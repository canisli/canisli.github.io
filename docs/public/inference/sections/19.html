18. Conjugate Priors

So far:

  • Least informative prior chooses weights over models such that observing is maximally informative about which model generated it.

    • The name is ironic: it refers to the fact that it’s hardest for nature to exploit against you in the minimax game, and in the Bayesian sense it’s the most informative prior.
  • It’s hard to compute, and we expoit the flexibility of weights to compute the maximally ignorant prior. But this may not be easy to update with new beliefs.
  • Hence we develop conjugate priors.

It says that as N → ∞, the posterior distribution converges to a Gaussian centered at the true parameter with variance equal to 1/(N · Fisher information), regardless of the prior (provided the prior has support at the true value). So asymptotically, two different priors give you the same posterior — the data completely washes out the prior’s influence.

  • And then in the small data regime, you choose least informative / maximally ignorant prior.
