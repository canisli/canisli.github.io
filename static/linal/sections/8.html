8. Infinite-dimensional vector spaces

8.1. All vector spaces have a basis

Refer to https://dept.math.lsa.umich.edu/~kesmith/infinite.pdf which is very well written. Also see https://gowers.wordpress.com/2008/08/12/how-to-use-zorns-lemma/, by Mr. IMO perfect score.

Hamel basis is an infinite basis that spans a vector space, where by “span” we mean finite linear combinations. These exist by Zorn’s lemma but are hard to find. Kind of like how Banach-Tarski proves possibility of building a second sphere but not how to.

An alternate formulation of basis permits infinite linear combinations, as long as they are unique. We need an orthonormal basis for this, such as the Fourier basis. It’s easy to show that the Fourier basis is orthonormal, but how do we know it spans all functions.

Hilbert spaces are complete inner product spaces. The vector space of all square integratable continuous functions on an interval is the completion of the vector space of all continuous functions (Can you think of an example of a Cauchy sequence that doesn’t converge here).
